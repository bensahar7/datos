# Datos Health — HIPA Product: Customer Research Synthesis

**Date:** May 27, 2026  
**Research mode:** Digital watering hole + public asset analysis  
**Product:** Datos Health Open Care Platform — Hospital at Home (HIPA/HiTH) programs  
**Note:** Datos Health has virtually zero public customer reviews (0 reviews on Capterra, no G2 profile found). This report is synthesized from partner testimonials, case study references, press releases, industry reports, and broader HaH market research.

---

## Top Themes (ranked by frequency × intensity)

### Theme 1: Customizability & Clinical Autonomy
**Summary:** Datos Health's strongest signal is that clinicians value the ability to build and modify care workflows themselves — without IT dependency. The no-code Design Studio and open platform model are consistently highlighted.  
**Frequency:** Appeared in 5+ sources (partner quotes, product pages, press releases)  
**Intensity:** High — language like "exactly what we need," "only company to offer us"  
**Confidence:** Medium (sourced from curated testimonials, not independent reviews)

**Representative quotes:**
- *"Datos was the only company to offer us exactly what we need to prepare..."* — Prof. Eyal Zimlichman, Sheba Medical Center
- *"We were able to personalize the Datos Health app for our research study of..."* — Dr. Himabindu Vidula, University of Rochester
- *"Datos Health offered the only digital health solution that met the..."* — Beat Knusel, Amgen

**Implications:** Positioning should lead with clinical team empowerment and no-code flexibility. This is Datos' clearest differentiator vs. rigid pre-built RPM competitors.

---

### Theme 2: Device Agnosticism & Integration Flexibility
**Summary:** Unlike competitors that lock customers into specific hardware, Datos integrates with any connected device — including devices patients already own (smartwatches, etc.). This matters for compliance and reducing logistics burden.  
**Frequency:** Appears in product marketing, partnership announcements, and competitive positioning  
**Intensity:** Medium — functional differentiator, less emotionally charged  
**Confidence:** Medium (vendor-stated, partially validated by partner integrations with Pulsenmore, Nuvo, Vaica)

**Implications:** For health systems already invested in devices, Datos removes a switching barrier. For patients, it reduces friction. This is a strong point for enterprise sales conversations.

---

### Theme 3: Clinical Outcomes (Cardiac Rehab — Sheba Medical Center)
**Summary:** The most concrete published outcome data comes from a tele-cardiac rehabilitation study at Sheba Medical Center showing measurable improvement in exercise capacity, therapy adherence, and patient satisfaction.  
**Frequency:** Single published study (IMAJ journal)  
**Intensity:** Medium-High — quantified results  
**Confidence:** Medium (single study, specific clinical domain)

**Key metrics:**
- 63%+ of patients met the goal of 150 min/week aerobic exercise
- ~40% increase in patients reaching target medication dose (ARNI, SGLT2i, Beta B., MRA)
- 80%+ program adherence per ESC guidelines
- Patient satisfaction: **4.05 / 5.0**

**Implications:** This is the strongest proof point Datos has. But it's cardiac rehab — not a broad HaH outcome. More published studies across clinical domains would significantly strengthen the platform's credibility.

---

### Theme 4: Market Positioning Gap — Missing from Key Industry Reviews
**Summary:** Datos Health was **not included** in the Prevounce 2026 RPM vendor comparison (which featured Biofourmis, Current Health, and Inbound Health for Hospital at Home). Datos also has zero public reviews on Capterra, G2, or Software Advice.  
**Frequency:** Consistent absence across all major review platforms  
**Intensity:** High (negative signal) — for enterprise buyers who rely on peer reviews, this is a trust gap  
**Confidence:** High

**Implications:** This is a significant go-to-market weakness. Enterprise buyers in the US increasingly rely on G2/KLAS/Capterra for shortlisting. The absence suggests either: (a) Datos' customer base is primarily non-US (Israel, Australia), (b) their GTM hasn't prioritized review generation, or (c) their customer count is still small. Any feature work or pitch should account for this credibility gap.

### Theme 5: Broader HaH Market Pain Points (Category-Level Research)
**Summary:** Clinicians across all HaH platforms consistently report the same frustrations. These are not Datos-specific but represent the problem space any Datos feature must address.  
**Frequency:** Appeared in 4+ independent industry sources (AHRQ, JMIR, Springer, Healthcare IT News)  
**Intensity:** High — described as burnout factors  
**Confidence:** High (multi-source, independent research)

**Top clinician pain points in HaH programs:**

| Pain Point | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Alert fatigue** | Threshold-based alerts overwhelm clinicians with noise; too many false positives |
| **Dual-system burden** | Logging into RPM platform AND EMR separately creates friction and errors |
| **Device skepticism** | Some clinicians distrust accuracy of home medical devices |
| **Data overload without triage** | Patient-generated data grows faster than clinical capacity to review it |
| **Logistics complexity** | Device provisioning, shipping, refurbishment is operationally heavy |
| **Protocol rigidity** | Pre-built programs don't match real clinical workflows |

**Emerging solutions the market is moving toward:**
- Exception-based workflows (automation handles routine; only escalates anomalies)
- AI-driven triage and predictive deterioration detection
- Integrated EMR workflows to eliminate dual-system problem
- Passive/continuous monitoring to reduce patient burden

## Jobs to Be Done (Synthesized from all sources)

### Functional Jobs
1. **Monitor high-acuity patients at home** with the same clinical oversight as inpatient care
2. **Customize care workflows** to match existing clinical protocols without IT involvement
3. **Integrate multiple device data streams** into a single dashboard with actionable alerts
4. **Reduce 30-day readmissions** through early detection and intervention
5. **Automate routine patient engagement** (reminders, education, questionnaires) to free clinical time

### Emotional Jobs
1. **Feel confident** that home patients are as safe as inpatients
2. **Feel empowered** to design care programs that reflect clinical expertise, not vendor limitations
3. **Reduce anxiety** about missing a deteriorating patient among dozens being monitored remotely

### Social Jobs
1. **Demonstrate innovation leadership** to hospital administration and peers
2. **Show measurable ROI** (30-50% cost reduction) to justify program investment
3. **Be seen as patient-centered** by offering care in the comfort of home

## Persona Sketch (Based on Available Data)

### Dr. Clinical Champion — Cardiologist or Dept. Head at a Mid-Large Health System

**Profile**
- Title: Department Head, Medical Director, or Clinical Informaticist
- Organization: Academic medical center or large health system (500+ beds)
- Reports to: CMO or VP of Clinical Operations
- Geography: Israel, Australia, or US East Coast

**Primary JTBD:** "Enable my patients to recover safely at home while maintaining the clinical oversight I'd have if they were in my unit."

**Trigger Events:**
- CMS waiver expansion / reimbursement incentive for HaH
- Post-COVID mandate to expand virtual care capacity
- Readmission penalty pressure from payers
- Competitor health system launching a HaH program

**Top Pains:**
1. "I can't customize the care plan to match how we actually practice"
2. "I'm drowning in alerts that don't mean anything"
3. "I have to log into two different systems just to see one patient's data"

**Why They Choose Datos:**
- No-code flexibility lets them build programs their way
- Device agnosticism means they're not locked into hardware decisions
- Platform supports multiple clinical domains from one system

**Objections:**
- "Who else in the US is using this?" (credibility gap)
- "Where are the published outcomes beyond cardiac rehab?"
- "How does this integrate with our Epic/Cerner?"
