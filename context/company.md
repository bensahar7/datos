# Datos Health — Company Profile

## Who They Are

Datos Health is an Israeli digital health company, founded in 2015, that builds an AI-driven hybrid care platform for remote patient monitoring and automated care delivery. The company was born from a personal experience: founder Uri Bettesh's father-in-law, a diagnosed cardiac patient, required emergency open-heart surgery — prompting Bettesh to ask why chronic deterioration hadn't been detected earlier through continuous monitoring. That question became the product: a no-code platform that lets clinicians design, deploy, and automate remote care workflows across specialties, integrating wearable and medical device data with algorithmic triage and escalation. Headquartered in Tel Aviv, with offices in New York and Sydney, Datos positions itself as a replacement for rigid, pre-built RPM solutions.

## Mission

- Quoted: **"On a Mission to Democratize Health Care"**
- The company frames its value proposition around the idea that digital opportunities should expand clinical reach, break down physical boundaries, and deliver remote care through "true automation, true customization, true flexibility" — specifically solving the problem that most digital health tools create more clinical work rather than reducing it.

## What the Product Does

1. **OpenCare Platform (Design Studio)** — A no-code workflow builder that lets clinicians create and customize remote care pathways. Mechanically, it translates clinical protocols into automated decision trees that trigger device data collection, triage logic, escalation alerts, and patient-facing content. No performance stats publicly available.

2. **ReadyCare Library** — 300+ pre-built clinical pathways, ready to deploy out of the box. Covers Hospital at Home, Cardiac Rehabilitation, Oncology Care, High-Risk Pregnancy, CHF, Diabetes Management, Pulmonary Rehabilitation, and Mental Health. Designed to reduce time-to-go-live for new programs.

3. **Patient CareApps** — Device-agnostic mobile applications that collect biometrics and PROMs/PREMs, deliver personalized educational content, medication reminders, and self-care guidance. Serves as the patient-facing layer of any deployed workflow.

4. **Automated Triage & Escalation** — Algorithmic layer that validates patient-generated health data, detects anomalies, and either routes to self-care or escalates to the care team with clinical context. Aims to reduce alert fatigue by filtering signal from noise.

5. **Virtual Visits** — Integrated telehealth capability embedded within care workflows, triggered contextually (e.g., when triage logic determines a live consultation is needed). Recognized by AVIA as "Best in Virtual Visits 2024."

6. **AI Care** — Workflow and outcome optimization tools layered on top of the platform. Specific capabilities and performance metrics not publicly detailed.

## Business Model

- **Sales motion:** Enterprise / health-system-level sale. Department-level entry appears common (e.g., cardiology or oncology departments deploying first, then expanding).
- **Who buys vs. who uses:** Purchased by health system leadership (CIOs, VPs of Digital Health, CMOs). Used by clinical care teams (nurses, physicians, care coordinators) and patients.
- **Key purchase language:** Elimination of rigid RPM tools; no-code customization; reduction of clinical workload (not addition); rapid go-live with pre-built pathways; compliance with hospital-at-home mandates.
- **TAM:** Not publicly disclosed. The adjacent RPM market is broadly sized at $5-8B (2025) by various analyst firms, but Datos has not published a company-specific TAM figure.
- **Pricing model:** Not publicly available.

## Regulatory or Market Tailwinds

- **CMS Hospital at Home Waiver (Acute Hospital Care at Home program):** CMS expanded reimbursement for hospital-level care delivered at home. Datos' Hospital at Home pathway maps directly to this — health systems need a platform to operationalize the waiver.
- **CMS RPM/RTM reimbursement codes (CPT 99453-99458, 98975-98981):** Established billing codes for remote patient monitoring and remote therapeutic monitoring create a revenue pathway for health systems using Datos' platform.
- **Post-COVID shift to hybrid care delivery:** The permanent behavioral shift toward virtual-first care (accelerated 2020-2022) sustains demand for platforms that blend remote monitoring with in-person escalation.
- **Value-based care transition:** As payers and health systems shift from fee-for-service to outcomes-based contracts, tools that reduce readmissions and ED visits through proactive monitoring become cost-justified.
- **Israel's national digital health push:** Israel's Ministry of Health is actively digitizing care delivery across its 24 hospitals — Datos was selected as a platform partner for this initiative.
