# Datos Health — Market & Competitive Context

## The problem in numbers
- $300B+ wasted annually in the US on avoidable healthcare utilization, much of it due to lack of remote monitoring and care continuity (McKinsey, 2024)
- ~80% of chronic disease management happens outside the hospital — yet most health systems lack tools to manage it
- Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) adoption among US hospitals grew from 29% (2019) to 67% (2024), yet most implementations are siloed, single-condition, and hard to customize
- Provider burnout remains a top concern — 60%+ of clinicians report EHR/workflow burden as a major driver

## Market size

**Remote Patient Monitoring (RPM) — Datos's core market:**
- $71.9B globally in 2024
- Projected $238B+ by 2032
- CAGR: ~16%

**Virtual Care / Telehealth (broader category):**
- $90B+ globally in 2024
- Projected $350B+ by 2032
- CAGR: ~18%

**Care Orchestration / Clinical Workflow Automation (emerging category):**
- $12B+ globally in 2024, growing rapidly as health systems move from point-solution RPM to platform-based care design
- Analysts increasingly separate "care orchestration platforms" from basic RPM or EHR add-ons

**Digital health funding context:**
- US digital health startups raised $14.2B in 2025 (up from $10.5B in 2024)
- AI-focused firms captured 54% of all digital health funding in 2025 (up from 37% in 2024)
- Average deal size: $29.3M in 2025 (up from $20.7M in 2024)

## Competitive landscape

### Direct competitors (care orchestration / RPM platforms)

| Company | Model | Key fact |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Current Health (Best Buy Health)** | Enterprise RPM platform with wearables + clinical monitoring | Acquired by Best Buy; strong in Hospital at Home; hardware-dependent model |
| **Biofourmis** | AI-powered RPM + virtual care platform | Pivoted from pharma to provider-facing; FDA-cleared algorithms; raised $300M+ |
| **Health Recovery Solutions (HRS)** | Telehealth & RPM for post-acute and chronic care | Strong in SNF/home health; more narrow than Datos's open platform approach |
| **Rimidi** | Clinical management platform for chronic conditions | Focused on specific conditions (diabetes, hypertension); less of a no-code/open platform |
| **Optimize Health** | RPM-as-a-service for practices and health systems | More turnkey/managed service; less customizable than Datos |

### Indirect competitors (EHR-embedded or adjacent)

| Company | Model | Key fact |
|---------|-------|----------|
| **Epic (MyChart / Care Companion)** | EHR-native RPM and patient engagement | Incumbency advantage; limited flexibility outside Epic ecosystem |
| **Teladoc / Livongo** | Virtual care + chronic condition management | Large scale; employer/payer-focused; less hospital workflow customization |
| **Philips (Patient Engagement)** | Connected care and monitoring devices + platform | Hardware-first; enterprise sales cycles; less agile |
| **Amwell** | Virtual care infrastructure | Broad telehealth; less depth in care orchestration and workflow automation |

### Key market dynamics
- Consolidation is accelerating — Best Buy acquired Current Health, Teladoc merged with Livongo, indicating category maturation
- Health systems are shifting from buying point-solution RPM to seeking **platforms** that support multiple conditions and workflows from a single tool
- "No-code" and clinician-configurable design is an emerging differentiator — most competitors require IT/vendor involvement to change protocols

## Datos's differentiation vs. the field
1. **No-code Design Studio** — clinicians can build and modify care pathways without engineering support. Most competitors require vendor professional services to customize workflows.
2. **300+ ReadyCare pathways** — pre-built clinical programs across 15+ specialties reduce time-to-value vs. building from scratch.
3. **Device-agnostic** — works with any connected device, not locked to proprietary hardware (unlike Current Health, Philips).
4. **Hybrid care model** — supports the full spectrum from fully automated self-care to clinician-directed virtual visits within one platform.
5. **Enterprise reference customers** — Kaiser Permanente, Mayo Clinic, Sheba Medical Center signal credibility with large health systems.

## Regulatory & reimbursement context
- **CMS RPM codes (99453, 99454, 99457, 99458)**: Established reimbursement pathway for remote patient monitoring — creates direct revenue opportunity for health systems using Datos.
- **Hospital at Home waivers**: CMS Acute Hospital Care at Home waiver (extended through 2024–2025) enables hospital-level care delivery at home, a key use case for Datos.
- **Value-based care shift**: ACOs, bundled payments, and STAR ratings all incentivize the kind of proactive, remote care management Datos enables.
- **Interoperability mandates**: ONC/CMS interoperability rules (TEFCA, information blocking) favor open, API-driven platforms over closed systems.
