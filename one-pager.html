Dimer's clinical model is already proactive — admins call within 12 hours, Transitionists follow up within 24. But between those calls, Dimer is blind. If a patient doesn't open the app, there is zero digital signal about how they're doing. The only reason 90% of patients never use AiME: it waits for them to show up first.

The Problem

A proactive clinical model on a reactive digital foundation

AiME is a powerful tool — but it's fully reactive. Patients must download the app, remember to open it, and think of a question. Patients most at risk of readmission (elderly, post-surgical, living alone) are the least likely to clear all three hurdles. Meanwhile, Cura already assigns risk scores to every patient — but Transitionists have no prioritized work queue to act on that intelligence. The signal exists. It just never surfaces.

The Solution

Two surfaces. One closed loop.

ProActive Loop pairs a frictionless daily patient check-in with a Cura-powered Transitionist dashboard. Instead of waiting for patients to open the app, AiME initiates a daily push notification — one tap to confirm "I'm okay." On the clinician side, silence and risk score combine into a prioritized queue: one screen that answers "who do I call first this morning?" in under 30 seconds.

The two surfaces

Patient: Daily Check-In

A personalized push notification between 8–10am — not a generic reminder. It references the patient's recovery day and procedure. One tap to respond. No app-opening required. Silence after 48 hours automatically elevates the patient's risk priority in the dashboard. Designed for the 72-year-old living alone, not the 30-year-old who uses ten apps a day.

Transitionist: Priority Dashboard

A Cura-powered work queue that answers one question every morning: who do I call first? Patients ranked by composite risk score + silence duration. Silent patients pinned to the top. Click any row to see 7 days of signal history, last AiME conversation, and Cura risk context — without leaving the queue. One screen. Under 30 seconds.

Why now

Series A momentum

Dimer's Series A announcement explicitly set the direction: "interactive, dynamic, responsive, personalized, connected, always available, and in real time." ProActive Loop is the most direct expression of that vision achievable in the next 6 months.

Why this

Builds on what exists

No new AI model required. Cura already scores patients — this feature routes that score into a UI. AiME already handles escalation — this feature adds the trigger layer. The infrastructure exists. The surface doesn't.

Why it scales

Headcount-independent growth

Transitionists work smarter, not more. More patient signals without more calls. Earlier detection without more staff. Every additional tap is a data point that could prevent a $15,000 readmission — and costs Dimer nothing to collect.
